Our Team

With a diverse skill set and a proven track record of meeting key objectives within the automotive industry we are committed to offering a service and ownership experience which exceeds expectations. Driven by passion, a demanding philosophy and sheer focused hard work, BAC has a comprehensive and coherent strategy to serve as a guide for all its activities. ‘Mono’ is the very embodiment of our approach; purity of design, innovative engineering, zero compromise and ultra-high performance.

The Total Ownership Experience of the BAC Mono is in many ways unparalleled. With Mono, we will introduce owners to a new benchmark in overall satisfaction by virtue of the quality of the product, attitude, and service proffered by our company. BAC’s team knows each of their customers by name. This is the foundation of an engaging emotional connection to a tribe in which owners are both welcome and feel a custodial sense of pride and involvement. Mono speaks to the driver that has always wanted a more intimate and visceral connection to the car. Only Mono is the answer to the childhood, teenage, and adult dream of being at the controls of a formula car.

Concept

Introducing Mono, a lightweight, ultra high performance, road legal supercar designed and manufactured using the latest racing technology. Aimed at drivers who seek a purist driving experience and for whom design, engineering excellence and performance are of paramount importance, Mono is an aggressive design and will set a new benchmark in the small, but elite segment of radical, lightweight sports cars.

Listen to an Interview with BAC Director, Ian Briggs explaining the concept behind Mono.

Design

The single central-seat gave our designers and project engineers the ultimate blank canvas on which to assemble their dream. The organic, enclosed front and exposed mechanical rear typifies the unique appearance of Mono. The design aesthetic is intended to combine sculpted, sleek, organic forms to provide an emotional, human connection juxtaposing the technical, mechanical elements of the car.

While this vision runs throughout Mono's appearance it was fundamental to our designers that form follow function on all aspects of the car. The necessity to gain access to the central driving position and adjustable dampers as well as providing clean airflow to the radiators and engine are just some of the factors on which Mono's architecture is built.

Location

Based in Liverpool within easy access of local amenities and the thriving city centre, BAC headquarters is the birthplace of each and every Mono and where enthusiasts from all over the world go to ensure their unique supercar is just the way they want it.

The BAC team prides itself on being talented, passionate and responsible across all aspects of the business, and its professionalism has contributed hugely to BAC’s immense success. In the Mono, BAC has created one of the most exciting and celebrated British products this side of the Millennium, giving every member of staff a great sense of pride at being associated with the car. Like the Mono, the BAC team is made up of many different components that pull together to make something very, very special.

Ian Briggs - Design Director

Ian is an accomplished designer with a reputation for innovative and radical concepts that have won him awards and accolades in diverse fields including aviation, luxury yachts and the automotive sector. He and his brother Neill formed the consulting venture Adaptive Space in 2000 and have worked with major OEMs (primarily Porsche, Mercedes and Audi) for over a decade, along with clients such as Lufthansa and its Airbus A380 fleet. As co-founder of BAC Ltd alongside his brother, his remit with Mono makes him responsible for program creative design direction from concept through to production implementation, active in all creative aspects of component, system and total vehicle design, exterior and interior.

Neill Briggs - Director of Product Development

As an engineer Neill has vast experience across the motor industry, having worked for the likes of Bentley and Ford, where he was Engineering Manager. Neill led the team responsible for the award winning “Multi-link, control blade, rear suspension” on the Focus RS, a car that proved to be a game changer for Ford. As co-founder of BAC (Briggs Automotive Company) Ltd his current brief sees him responsible for the entire program delivery and management of all disciplines within the business, active in all aspects of component, system and total vehicle design, development and engineering. For Neill, BAC is the realisation of a life-long ambition to develop innovative vehicles that show what is possible when we question and challenge convention.

Murray Adams - Senior Designer

Murray joined the team in 2007 having graduated from Pforzheim’s Masters in Transportation design course in Germany. Since project inception he has worked extensively - from initial concept through to production implementation - on all aspects of the interior and exterior design of Mono, including initial concept/developmental sketches, clay model and CAD realisation. Oliver Webb – Test and Development Driver

Despite his modest years, professional racing driver Oliver Webb brings great experience to his role as Mono test and development driver. A winner since he first stepped into a kart, Oliver has competed in a variety of Formula over the world’s greatest race circuits. Most recently he won the 2014 European Le Mans Series Championship with Signatech Alpine, a season that featured a 7th place overall finish in his first Le Mans 24 race. Oliver has been instrumental in the setup of Mono, helping it to be formidable as both a road and track car. In addition to his driving role Oliver has also provided valuable feedback to the videogames which feature Mono, most notably Project Cars, where he has been involved since the games inception, ensuring that the experience will be extremely realistic.
